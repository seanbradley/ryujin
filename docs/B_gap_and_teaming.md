# DICE -- Capability / Gap Analysis and Teaming Memo

> BAA HR001126S0010 (DICE). Honest feasibility assessment for a TA1+TA2 bid
> built on the Duchaine / STYG-ENGINE control kernel. Audience: principal.
> This memo is deliberately unsentimental per our own anti-sycophancy doctrine.

## 1. Bottom line

The *idea* is fundable and differentiated. The *organization, as it stands
today, is not a credible prime* for a 36-month DARPA fundamental-research award
in distributed consensus and inference-time control. The viable path is a
**teaming play** where we supply the TA2 control-theory kernel + doctrine
narrative and a research partner supplies consensus / interpretability weight.

Recommended posture: **submit the abstract (low cost, high information), pursue
a partner in parallel, and only commit to the full proposal if a credible PI is
secured by mid-July 2026.**

## 2. What we have (genuine assets)

- **A novel, defensible TA2 thesis.** "Rigid contracts, flexible execution" +
  conservation laws + infeasibility surfacing + anti-sycophancy is a real,
  empirically-grounded answer to long-horizon role drift -- DICE's stated
  TA2 problem. We discovered and named the silent-divergence failure mode.
- **A bounded-stigmergy concept for TA1.** Reinforcement strictly beneath an
  inviolable heuristic layer is a legitimate self-organization-without-entropy
  story, directly responsive to the ClawBots/Moltbook entropy framing.
- **Working intuition for the failure modes** from having shipped (and debugged)
  a centrally-orchestrated DAG -- i.e., we have lived the baseline DICE wants to
  beat. Useful for credibility, not as the architecture.
- **A doctrine/narrative voice** (OODA, OPORD, "remaining under control") that
  fits the program's framing unusually well.

## 3. What we lack (the gaps)

| Gap | Severity | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| PI-grade research credentials in distributed consensus / BFT | HIGH | Step-2 "Technical Qualifications"; reviewers expect publication record |
| Mechanistic interpretability + activation steering expertise | HIGH | Core TA2 mechanism for open-weight agents |
| Track record running large agentic simulations / HPC | MED | Phase-3 scale (100K agents) credibility |
| Formal-methods / game-theory depth | MED | BAA names these as the disciplines in scope |
| SAM.gov registration + UEI | MED | Required to receive award; multi-week lead time |
| CMMC posture (procurement contract path) | LOW-MED | Avoidable by proposing OT for Research |
| Cost-proposal / DARPA contracting machinery | MED | Volume II, model-agreement red-lines, FRRBS forms |

The conceptual gap to the *Phase 1* bar is small. The staffing gap is the
binding constraint. The Phase-3 conceptual gap (O(n) at 100K agents, 10K-step
adversarial coherence) is real but is shared by every entrant -- it is the
research, not a disqualifier.

## 4. Teaming options (in rough order of preference)

1. **Be a TA2-led subcontractor / co-PI to an academic prime.** Lowest risk.
   A university lab with consensus + interpretability faculty primes TA1+TA2;
   we own the TA2 control kernel as a defined work package. Note: FFRDCs/UARCs
   are INELIGIBLE to propose (Sec. 8), so target a standard research university
   or an AI-safety lab, not an FFRDC/UARC/National Lab.
2. **Small-business prime + academic sub.** We prime (small-business framing is
   encouraged, Sec. 8), subcontract the consensus/interpretability research.
   Higher management burden; requires SAM + cost machinery on our side.
3. **Join an existing team forming around DICE** purely as the doctrine/control
   IP contributor. Least control, fastest path, smallest upside.

## 5. Honest odds (speculative)

- **P(abstract conforms + "Encouraged")** with a sharp, original write-up:
  *moderate-to-good* -- the thesis is genuinely non-boilerplate.
- **P(competitive full proposal) solo, no partner:** *low.* Technical
  Qualifications and Cost Realism would be thin.
- **P(competitive full proposal) with a credible research partner:** *fair* --
  the TA1<->TA2 coupling story is strong and many teams will under-address it.

These are extrapolations, not guarantees.
